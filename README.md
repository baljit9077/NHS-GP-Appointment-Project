# NHS-GP-Appointment-Project
This project shows a Power BI dashboard created using NHS GP appointment data from the “Appointments in General Practice” dataset. The data includes information about appointments such as whether patients attended or missed (DNA), how the appointment was done (face-to-face, phone, online), how long patients waited after booking, and the type of appointment (routine, acute, clinics, etc.). According to the NHS supporting information, this dataset is experimental, which means the data may not be perfect because different GP practices record information in different ways. Also, the data only shows recorded appointments and does not include full GP workload, patient demand, or available capacity.

The dashboard gives an overall view of GP appointment activity. It shows around 31 million total appointments, with about 28 million attended and around 1 million missed, giving a DNA rate of about 4.38%. A large number of appointments are same-day, which suggests that GP services deal with many urgent or immediate cases. The charts show that most appointments are attended, while a smaller number are missed or marked as unknown. The booking analysis shows that shorter waiting times are more common, and longer waiting times are linked to more missed appointments.

The dashboard also shows that face-to-face appointments are still the most common, followed by telephone appointments, while online appointments are used less. The category analysis shows that routine consultations make up the largest part of GP work, followed by acute cases and planned clinics. The dashboard also compares different practices and regions, showing that some practices have higher DNA rates, which may indicate service issues or access problems.

To build this dashboard, measures were created in Power BI using DAX, such as total appointments, total DNA, total attended appointments, DNA rate, and same-day appointments. These measures help to calculate accurate totals from the dataset, which is already grouped. The main insights from this project are that longer waiting times can lead to more missed appointments, and that the type and mode of appointment can affect patient attendance.

Overall, this project shows how NHS data can be analysed to understand appointment activity and patient behaviour. It helps identify patterns in attendance, waiting times, and service delivery, while also recognising the limitations of the data, such as incomplete coverage, data quality issues, and lack of information on demand and capacity.
